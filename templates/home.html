Have Questions About Medicare? We’re Here to Help

Understanding Medicare can be challenging. We’ve compiled answers to common questions to guide you through enrollment, benefits, and coverage options.

What is Medicare and who is eligible?

Medicare is a federal health insurance program primarily for individuals aged 65 and older, certain younger people with disabilities, and those with End-Stage Renal Disease.

How do I enroll in Medicare?

You can enroll through the Social Security Administration (SSA) website, by visiting a local SSA office, or by calling their toll-free number. Enrollment periods may vary depending on your circumstances.

What are the different parts of Medicare?

Medicare has four parts:

  • Part A: Hospital insurance
  • Part B: Medical insurance
  • Part C: Medicare Advantage Plans
  • Part D: Prescription drug coverage

What does Medicare not cover?

Medicare generally does not cover long-term care, dental care, eye exams for prescription glasses, hearing aids, and cosmetic surgery. Consider supplemental insurance for additional coverage.

How much does Medicare cost?

Costs vary by coverage type. Part A is usually free for those who paid Medicare taxes, but Parts B, C, and D often involve premiums, deductibles, and coinsurance.

How can I get help with Medicare costs?

Programs like Medicaid, Medicare Savings Programs, and Extra Help for prescription drug costs may provide financial assistance. Contact us at medicare@medicare.com or call +254 111 255301 for guidance.
